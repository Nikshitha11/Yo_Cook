Mirchi Salan Recipe

Mirchi Salan

Mirchi Salan is a delicious and spicy curry made with long green chilies simmered in a tangy, nutty gravy. It's a classic Hyderabadi side dish that pairs perfectly with biryani or plain rice.

Ingredients:

  • 8-10 large green chilies
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1/2 cup peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons grated coconut
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup tamarind pulp
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Dry roast peanuts, sesame seeds, and grated coconut until aromatic. Cool and blend into a smooth paste with water. Set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Fry the green chilies until slightly blistered. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add mustard and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook until aromatic.
  5. Add the prepared paste, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 5 minutes on low heat.
  6. Pour in water and tamarind pulp. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  7. Add the fried green chilies and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the gravy thickens.
  8. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with coriander leaves.
