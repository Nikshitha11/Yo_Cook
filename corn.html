Corn Ambode Recipe

Corn Ambode (Sweet Corn Vada)

Corn Ambode, also known as Sweet Corn Vada, is a delicious and crispy snack made from a mixture of sweet corn, spices, and gram flour. It is perfect for serving as a starter or a tea-time snack.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sweet corn (boiled and coarsely ground)
  • 1/4 cup chickpea flour (besan)
  • 1-2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon rice flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Fresh coriander leaves (finely chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the corn mixture:
    • Grind the boiled sweet corn coarsely in a blender or food processor. Do not make a smooth paste; it should be slightly chunky.
    • In a mixing bowl, add the ground corn, chickpea flour, rice flour, green chilies, asafoetida, turmeric powder, cumin seeds, coriander leaves, and salt. Mix everything well.
  2. Shape the dough:
    • Take a small portion of the dough and shape it into round balls or flat patties, pressing them lightly to form a vada-like shape.
  3. Fry the vadas:
    • Heat oil in a frying pan or kadai on medium heat.
    • Once the oil is hot, carefully slide the shaped vadas into the oil and fry them in batches, turning them occasionally, until they are golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove them from the oil and drain excess oil by placing them on a paper towel.
