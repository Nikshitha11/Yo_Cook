Ingredients:

  • For the Rice:
    • 1 cup basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
    • 2 cups water
    • 1 tablespoon ghee or oil
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 2-3 cloves
    • 1-2 green cardamom pods
    • 1 small cinnamon stick
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • Salt to taste
  • For Garnish (Optional):
    • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
    • Fried onions

Instructions:

  1. Rinse and Soak Rice:
    • Wash the basmati rice thoroughly until the water runs clear.
    • Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes and then drain the water.
  2. Cook the Rice:
    • Heat ghee or oil in a large pot or pressure cooker on medium heat.
    • Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and cumin seeds. Let them splutter for 30 seconds to release their aroma.
    • Add the soaked and drained rice to the pot and sautÃ© for 2-3 minutes, gently stirring to coat the rice with the spices.
    • Pour in the water and add salt to taste. Bring the water to a boil.
  3. Simmering the Rice:
    • Once the water comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it cook for about 10-12 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed.
    • If using a pressure cooker, cook on low heat for 2-3 whistles.
  4. Fluff the Rice:
    • After the rice is cooked, let it rest for 5 minutes before opening the lid.
    • Fluff the rice with a fork to separate the grains.
  5. Garnishing (Optional):
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and fried onions for added flavor and crunch.

Serving:
