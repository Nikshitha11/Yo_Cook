Chapathi Recipe

Soft and Fluffy Chapathi

Chapathi is a traditional Indian flatbread made with whole wheat flour. It is soft, healthy, and perfect to accompany any curry or vegetable dish. Here's how you can make it at home:

Ingredients:

  • For the Dough:
    • 2 cups whole wheat flour
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • Water as needed
    • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee (optional)
  • For Cooking:
    • Ghee or oil for cooking the chapathis

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Dough:
    • In a mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour and salt.
    • Slowly add water little by little and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and soft.
    • If you like, you can add a tablespoon of oil or ghee to make the dough softer and more pliable.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
  2. Rolling the Chapathis:
    • Divide the dough into small balls (about the size of a golf ball).
    • Roll each dough ball between your palms to make it smooth.
    • Using a rolling pin, roll each dough ball into a thin, round disc (about 6-8 inches in diameter).
    • Make sure the surface is lightly floured to prevent sticking.
  3. Cooking the Chapathis:
    • Heat a tawa or flat skillet over medium-high heat.
    • Once the tawa is hot, place the rolled-out chapathi on it.
    • Cook the chapathi until small bubbles begin to form on the surface. Flip it over and cook the other side until lightly golden.
    • Press gently with a cloth or spatula to ensure it puffs up.
    • Brush ghee or oil on both sides and cook for an additional 10-20 seconds until crispy and golden.
  4. Serving:
    • Serve the chapathis hot with curry, dal, or a vegetable dish of your choice.

Enjoy!
