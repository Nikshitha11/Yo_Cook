Mirchi Bajji Recipe

Mirchi Bajji

Mirchi Bajji, also known as stuffed mirchi bajji, is a spicy and crispy Indian snack made by stuffing large green chilies with a spiced filling and then dipping them in a batter and deep-frying them. This dish is typically served with chutneys and is a popular street food in India.

Ingredients:

  • For the stuffing:
    • 8-10 large green chilies
    • 1/2 cup besan (gram flour)
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
    • 1/2 teaspoon amchur powder (dry mango powder)
    • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
    • Salt to taste
    • Chopped coriander leaves (optional)
  • For the batter:
    • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • Salt to taste
    • Water (to make the batter)
  • For frying:
    • Oil (for deep frying)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the stuffing:
    • Slit the green chilies lengthwise and remove the seeds carefully.
    • In a bowl, mix the besan, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, amchur powder, ginger-garlic paste, and salt.
    • Stuff the prepared mixture inside each slit chili, making sure the filling is packed well.
  2. Prepare the batter:
    • In a separate bowl, mix besan, baking soda, red chili powder, cumin seeds, and salt.
    • Add water little by little to make a smooth, thick batter that coats the chilies evenly.
  3. Fry the Mirchi Bajji:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan or wok over medium heat.
    • Dip each stuffed chili in the batter, making sure it's fully coated, and drop them carefully into the hot oil.
    • Fry the chilies in batches, turning them occasionally until they are golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove the bajjis from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
