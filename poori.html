Poori Recipe

Fluffy and Golden Poori

Poori is a popular Indian deep-fried bread, crispy on the outside and soft inside. It's perfect for breakfast or a festive meal. Here's how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1/2 teaspoon oil or ghee (optional, for softer dough)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine wheat flour and salt.
    • Gradually add water and knead into a stiff but pliable dough.
    • Add oil or ghee for extra softness, if desired.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15â€“20 minutes.
  2. Rolling the Pooris:
    • Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls.
    • Roll each ball into a smooth circle, about 4â€“5 inches in diameter. Use minimal flour for dusting to prevent sticking.
  3. Frying the Pooris:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat until hot.
    • Carefully slide one poori into the hot oil. It should puff up within a few seconds.
    • Gently press with a slotted spoon to help it puff evenly.
    • Flip and fry the other side until golden brown.
    • Remove the poori and drain on a paper towel. Repeat with the remaining dough.
  4. Serving:
    • Serve hot pooris with aloo curry, chole (chickpea curry), or your favorite side dish.
