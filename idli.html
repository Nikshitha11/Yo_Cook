Soft and Fluffy Idli

Idli is a traditional South Indian steamed rice cake, soft and fluffy, perfect for breakfast. Here's how you can make it at home:

Ingredients:

  • For Batter:
    • 1 cup idli rice
    • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
    • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional, for flavor)
    • Salt to taste
    • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • For Steaming:
    • Oil or ghee (for greasing the idli molds)

Instructions:

  1. Soaking Ingredients:
    • Wash and rinse idli rice and urad dal separately 2â€“3 times to remove impurities.
    • Soak idli rice and urad dal (along with fenugreek seeds, if using) in separate bowls for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  2. Grinding the Batter:
    • Drain the water from the soaked ingredients.
    • In a blender or wet grinder:
      • Grind urad dal with a little water until it becomes smooth and fluffy. Transfer it to a large mixing bowl.
      • Grind the idli rice coarsely with a little water (the consistency should be slightly grainy, not too fine).
    • Mix the ground rice and urad dal together in the mixing bowl. Add salt and mix thoroughly.
  3. Fermenting the Batter:
    • Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8â€“10 hours, or until it doubles in volume. (Fermentation time may vary depending on the climate.)
    • After fermentation, gently mix the batter without deflating it.
  4. Preparing the Idli Molds:
    • Grease the idli molds with a little oil or ghee.
    • Pour the fermented batter into the molds, filling them about 3/4 full.
  5. Steaming the Idlis:

    • Steam on medium heat for 10â€“12 minutes. (Do not open the lid during steaming.)
    • Check if the idlis are done by inserting a toothpick; it should come out clean.

    • Use a spoon or knife to gently remove the idlis from the molds.
