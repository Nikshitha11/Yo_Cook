Gobi Manchurian Recipe

Gobi Manchurian

Gobi Manchurian is a popular Indo-Chinese dish made with crispy cauliflower florets tossed in a flavorful, spicy, and tangy sauce. It's perfect as an appetizer or served with fried rice or noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium cauliflower (gobi), cut into florets
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup cornflour
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed (for batter)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 2 tablespoons oil (for sauce)
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup chopped bell peppers
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons tomato ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon chili sauce
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • Spring onions for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil water in a pot, add salt, and blanch the cauliflower florets for 2-3 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, cornflour, ginger-garlic paste, pepper powder, and salt. Add water to form a smooth batter.
  3. Heat oil for deep frying. Dip the cauliflower florets in the batter and fry until golden and crisp. Set aside.
  4. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil. SautÃ© onions, green chilies, and bell peppers for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, chili sauce, vinegar, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the fried cauliflower florets to the sauce and toss to coat evenly.
  7. Garnish with spring onions and serve hot.
